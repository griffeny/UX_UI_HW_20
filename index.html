Knots of Love Project

My role in this project was as a UX Researcher and Product Manager. I managed communication with the CEO of Knots of Love and led the charge as we conducted research via interviews and surveys.

The Company and the Problem

Knots of Love is a non-profit organization that provides handmade beanies for chemo patients. KOL depends on their volunteers to make and send them the beanies on a weekly basis.
The CEO had a few things she wanted to improve on their website:

  1. They wanted their colors to showcase hope to those who visit their site
  2. They needed a mobile responsive site
  3. They wanted their site to be easier to navigate
Founder

We spoke with Knots of Love founder Christine Fabiani who expressed frustration with her current website. She said she wanted the site to be “bright and hopeful”. This is such a difficult experience for people and hopefulness and positivity is something that is necessary for the website.

She also mentioned “organization” was one of the main things she wanted improved on the website. She felt that the header and navigation bar were too cluttered. Research

~90% of responders are adults between the ages of 25-45. Many of which may not have enough time to donate their skills while balancing a busy work schedule.

Volunteering and attending events are also favorable ways to “Get Involved”

Social media was the most popular way to stay up to date on non-profit information, followed by word of mouth and subscribing to their newsletter.

Monetary donations was the most selected type of interaction regarding non-profit or charities.

User Interview Insights

My team started addressing their needs by checking the painpoints of their website visitors. We conducted user testing and a survey to see things from the user’s perspective and from there we moved onto our plan.

The insight from the user tests helped inform the user flow that should be used.

We used those insights to create an affinity diagram and prioritization matrix as well.
